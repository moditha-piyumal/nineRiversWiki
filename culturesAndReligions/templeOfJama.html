The History and Significance!

The Temple of Jama was designed and built by Joon Krad and Joon Rarwa a thousand years ago. It is known in the vague historical records that after a great holy war between two conflicting versions of the Faith of Jamapada, the Jamapada was carved into a stone by the victors aforementioned. The words carved on the massive stone slabs in this temple is regarded as the words of the Gods and the Joonists do not debate over the accuracy of the words. This is the greatest holy place of the Joonist people!

The Secrecy

Although the Joonists claim that the temple is located in a secret forest hideaway, this cannot be further from the truth. Foreigners visit this temple as well and there are many unpaved roads leading to the temple. The roads are probably unpaved because nobody has every tried to make a road to the so called "secret temple". However, thousands of pilgrims following the same path to a single location creates a road by the natural flow of time.

Great Stone Slabs

The Great Stone Slabs situated in a cave within this temple contains the accepted wordings of the sacred text of Jamapada. The Joonists consider this book, or rather the stone inscription to be holy. The inscription is being perfectly preserved from the elements of nature and there are many expert stonemasons who repair any words that may be subjected to natural wear and tear. There are scholars making copies of the Jamapada inscription on books.
